We present Thayer-Net, a controlled synthetic benchmark for galaxy deblending
with Galaxy10 DECaLS cutouts. The benchmark constructs foreground-only blends
with known clean targets, avoiding rectangular artifacts from naive cutout
addition. We compare identity and threshold baselines with compact U-Net models
trained for direct target reconstruction, residual contaminant prediction,
balanced hard-case residual prediction, and affected/core-weighted residual
prediction. Thayer-Direct improves affected-region mean squared error over
identity on normal held-out blends, but hard stress testing reveals weaker
robustness under stronger overlap and contaminant conditions. Thayer-Residual
improves stress performance by learning contaminant light to subtract.
Thayer-BR v0.1 shows that balanced hard-case training improves robustness, and
Thayer-BR v0.2 Moderate further improves affected-region and core affected
error with a weighted residual loss. In the current controlled benchmark,
Thayer-BR v0.2 Moderate reaches about 32.3x normal and 19.6x stress
affected-region improvement over identity, with multi-seed improvements of
32.02 +/- 1.21x and 19.55 +/- 0.30x. The results support controlled synthetic
deblending as a useful model-comparison setting, while remaining limited by
simplified sky, PSF, noise, source-environment, and apparent-size assumptions.
