Thayer-Net provides a controlled setting for studying compact U-Net models on
synthetic galaxy deblending. Direct reconstruction establishes a strong learned
baseline over identity and threshold methods. Stress testing exposes robustness
loss under harder overlap conditions. Residual prediction improves stress
robustness, balanced hard-case training improves it further, and moderate
affected/core-weighted residual loss gives the strongest current model family.

On exact-pixel and exact-coordinate grouped development data, the v0.2 Moderate
retrain remains strong, with 28.81x lower normal affected-region MSE than
identity and 15.80x lower hard-stress MSE. The old 32.3x result remains plausible
but is retained only as original row-index development context. The main
conclusion is that objective design, training distribution, and region-aware
loss weighting matter in this controlled RGB restoration setting.

No final-paper effect size is yet established. The next step is to freeze a
fresh untouched group-disjoint final partition after protocol freeze, add
clustered uncertainty and independent training seeds, and test physical-realism
and size-normalized controls before stronger size-invariant or real-sky claims.
