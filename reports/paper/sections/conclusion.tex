Thayer-Net provides a controlled setting for studying compact U-Net models on
synthetic galaxy deblending. Direct reconstruction establishes a strong learned
baseline over identity and threshold methods. Stress testing exposes robustness
loss under harder overlap conditions. Thayer-Residual improves stress
robustness, and Thayer-BR v0.1 gives the strongest aggregate affected-region
metrics in the current evaluation.

The main conclusion is that both model objective and training distribution
matter for controlled synthetic deblending. The next step is to write up the
current results carefully, preserve exact generated evaluation sets for future
audits, and extend realism only where it directly supports the scientific
question.
