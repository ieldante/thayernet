# Competing-hypothesis recoverability

The research question remains: when does a blended observation support a
trustworthy reconstruction of the requested galaxy, and when should the system
abstain?

This approach replaces one model's confidence with a finite challenge set.
Multiple candidate decompositions are mapped into one source-layer contract,
recomposed through the frozen observation model, and filtered by a
calibration-only noise tolerance. Scientifically important disagreement among
retained requested-source layers is an empirical reason to abstain.

The first campaign achieved a partial success. A 25-pair Ambiguity Atlas and
49/50 observation-level constructed witnesses show that genuinely different
requested galaxies can be supported within the same measurement tolerance.
All three frozen Thayer controls had at least one unsafe noisy reconstruction
on every Atlas pair. Candidate disagreement from those controls activated only
18/50 witnesses, so absence of model disagreement remains uninformative.

Only one meaningfully distinct compatible architecture cluster currently
exists. Cross-deblender auditing, catalog admission, safety-coverage curves,
and final-survey claims were not run. The exact next experiment is one
prospective compact prompted ResUNet trained once under the existing BTK
normalization and source-layer contract, followed only by the frozen Atlas
behavior audit and a renewed compatibility count.

## Atlas v0 superseding operational result

The stricter Atlas v0 run preserves the direct ambiguity result but fails the
operational detector gate. Constructed witnesses pass on 50/50 observations;
same-cluster model candidates pass on 19/50. Candidate diameter has AUROC
0.4712 and zero recall at the frozen 4% control false-positive rate, below the
narrow R1 confidence comparison. No black-box auditor is authorized.

## Stochastic candidate-family result

Thayer-PU supplies a compatible stochastic candidate family whose prior samples
are prompt-faithful and forward-consistent on protected non-Atlas data. On the
frozen Atlas, its candidate diameter improves operational discrimination to
AUROC 0.856 and 0.32 recall at 4% control false positives, while witnesses rise
to 24/50. Safe-control false witnesses remain 0.08.

The family is not truth-covering on Atlas: own and alternate coverage are both
zero under the frozen scientific metric. This blocks an auditor despite the
diameter improvement. The next experiment is one conditional normalizing-flow
prior correction, not a black-box policy or another deterministic U-Net.

## Flow-prior premise failed

The prospective flow-prior campaign audited the premise before fitting. Frozen
posterior samples covered neither own truths nor cross-decoded paired alternate
truths on protected non-Atlas data. A prior-only correction is therefore not
scientifically justified, and the flow and Atlas stages were not run. The one
next experiment is ambiguity-set decoder training under each approved
observationally equivalent pair condition.

## Shared set decoder failed truth coverage

Thayer-MH tested that continuation with 2,000 newly validated non-Atlas pairs
and an expanded 36,288-group exclusion. The shared token decoder remained
prompt-faithful and forward-consistent but covered neither own nor alternate
approved truths. It does not supply an operational candidate set, and
Thayer-Audit remains blocked.

## Independent experts failed the capacity gate

Thayer-ME reused the same approved scenes and target sets but gave each of two
experts independent decoder parameters. On the isolated microset, prompt swap
and forward consistency passed while all scientific truth-coverage rates
remained zero and ordinary outputs did not concentrate. The campaign stopped
before full training or Atlas. Parameter sharing alone is therefore not an
adequate explanation of the observed compromise under the current loss and
coverage geometry.

## Frozen loss geometry is misaligned

Thayer-LG audited the exact persisted objective without fitting a model or
opening the Atlas. Exact truths passed the output and coverage contracts, but
compromise beat truth on 84.375% of microset rows and on every ambiguous row.
The forward-to-observed term dominated truth-point gradients and conflicted
with source matching. Full-objective output optimization lowered loss while
destroying coverage, so forward consistency remains necessary but is not
source-identification correctness.
