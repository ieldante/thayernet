# Original reliability-contract postmortem

This postmortem reconstructs the exact Phase-II `moderate_actionable_success`
target from the persisted training, validation, and calibration rows. The
append-only row audit is in
`outputs/runs/thayer_select_hierarchical_safety_20260712_001405/`.

## Exact Boolean definition

For `VALID_SOURCE` and `PERTURBED_VALID`, the moderate contract succeeds only
when all of the following are true:

- evaluation output is finite and structurally valid;
- requested-source normalized RMSE is at most `0.75`;
- maximum per-band relative flux error is at most `0.30`;
- maximum `g-r`/`r-z` color error is at most `0.30` mag;
- centroid error is at most `2.0` pixels;
- source confusion is false;
- catastrophic failure is false.

Undefined required valid-query metrics fail closed. For `NULL_SOURCE`, the
underlying moderate outcome succeeds when evaluation is valid and neither the
10%-of-blend absolute-flux hallucination flag nor catastrophic failure is set.
For `AMBIGUOUS_SOURCE`, the underlying reconstruction contract always fails
because no unique requested-source truth exists.

The learned actionable composite is stricter in a logically important way:
it is the valid-query moderate success above, and is unconditionally zero for
all NULL and AMBIGUOUS requests. Thus a successful low-energy NULL outcome and
a hallucinating NULL outcome share the same learned negative class; all
ambiguous abstention cases, mild valid metric failures, source confusion, and
catastrophic valid failures are also collapsed into that class.

## Persisted-row result

The audit covered 10,000 training, 1,500 validation, and 2,000 calibration
rows. Reapplication produced zero underlying-contract mismatches and zero
actionable-label mismatches. Positive prevalence was 41/10,000 (`0.410%`) in
training, 5/1,500 (`0.333%`) in validation, and 30/2,000 (`1.500%`) in
calibration.

The negative class contained:

- 2,543 NULL rows that passed the underlying null-outcome contract but were
  correctly forced negative by the actionable definition;
- all 1,350 AMBIGUOUS rows, including 312 source-like forced candidates;
- 288 UNIQUE_VALID rows with exactly one non-catastrophic failed component;
- 7,505 catastrophic UNIQUE_VALID negatives.

This is not a Boolean implementation bug. It is a scientifically defective
target for direct recoverability learning because its negatives represent
different events with different applicability. The complete truth table,
per-row applicability masks and threshold distances, and failure-overlap table
are preserved in the corrective run. CSV readers must use
`keep_default_na=False` when reading the literal query-state token `NULL`.

## Provenance defect and corrective decision

Training and validation outcomes were generated by the frozen Condition-C
teacher, while calibration outcomes were generated by the Phase-II R1
candidate. The formula was identical but the reconstruction provenance was
not. This target-domain change, sparse positives, and heterogeneous semantics
prevent a clean attribution of the earlier calibration collapse.

The historical hierarchical campaign had already fitted heads and evaluated
development before the required preregistration and this postmortem existed.
Those steps cannot be retroactively preregistered. The corrective campaign
therefore stopped before new inference or training, made no policy changes,
did not reevaluate development, and did not access the lockbox.
