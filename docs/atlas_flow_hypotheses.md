# Atlas flow hypotheses

The proposed Thayer-PF hypothesis was that a compact conditional flow could
move inference-time probability mass toward posterior truth modes and improve
the frozen Thayer-PU Atlas result of 24/50 witnesses, AUROC 0.856, 0.32 recall at
4% control false positives, and zero own/alternate truth coverage.

The required non-Atlas premise failed before flow fitting. The frozen posterior
and decoder did not cover own truths, and cross-decoding did not represent paired
alternate truths. Consequently no flow checkpoint, prior sample sequence,
operating threshold, or one-time Atlas protocol was frozen. Atlas inference was
not authorized and the authoritative Thayer-PU metrics remain unchanged.

No Atlas claim about a flow prior is available. In particular, candidate
diameter remains an empirical ambiguity detector rather than evidence that its
sampled candidates represent plausible posterior truths. No auditor, catalog
admission policy, or final-lockbox evaluation follows from this campaign.
